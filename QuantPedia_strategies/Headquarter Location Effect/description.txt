Authors construct remoteness measures that characterize the geographic features of the firm, including the surrounding populations and distances from these population centers. Remotely headquartered stocks reportedly outperform proximate stocks by 8.59% annually on a risk-adjusted basis. We select that strategy using the simplest remoteness measure Remote6h as our database entry. The results confirm a remoteness premium. Fundamental reason
Results from this study add the missing piece between the literature on the role of firm headquarters geographic locations and the literature on the asset pricing implications of information frictions. The outperformance of remote stocks is from both the long and short legs and is highly persistent due to remoteness. Scientists also hypothesize that the market requires a positive premium for holding stocks with more remote headquarters due to the information frictions. This return predictability is mitigated by the geographic dispersion of firm operations, consistent with soft information frictions explaining the remoteness-return association. They also claim famous post-earnings announcement drift (hereafter PEAD), indicating delayed information incorporation, is only observed among remote firms.
